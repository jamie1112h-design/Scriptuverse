## Locked Architectural Decisions

### Decision 1 – Pipeline structure
Four-stage intake-to-output pipeline: Stage 0 (Emotional/Spiritual Check) leads to Layer 1 (Fixed Profile) leads to Layer 2 (Adaptive Dilemma Map) leads to Output Galaxy.

### Decision 2 – Stage 0
A single low-friction emotional/spiritual check precedes all profile questions. The heaviest responses route to crisis resources before any administrative intake begins. Anchor-phrase pacing logic from No More Mistakes is the reference pattern, to be reviewed against Scriptuverse's own framing once that spec is retrieved.

**Superseding note (Decision 23, this version):** Stage 0 no longer stands as a separate stage. It is merged into Layer 1 as the second question in the locked sequence ("How are you feeling about bringing this here right now?"), immediately after the opening tension question. The crisis-routing function described above is retained at that point in the flow; only the standalone-stage framing is superseded.

### Decision 3 – Layer 1 (Fixed Profile)
Six-question fixed intake from the June 2 draft (tradition/denomination, Bible version, life domain, what the user is seeking, tone preference, depth of response) stands as the working draft, subject to revision once Personal Support's Layer 1 pattern is reviewed.

**Superseding note (Decision 18, this version):** Q1's option set (the seven-category June 2 sketch — Evangelical/Mainline/Charismatic-Pentecostal/Catholic/Orthodox/Non-denominational/Other) is superseded by the Denomination and Tradition Taxonomy (v0.1). The six-question structure itself stands; Q1's actual content does not.

**Second superseding note (Decision 23, this version):** The six-question structure itself is now also superseded. Locked Layer 1 sequence, in order: an upfront framing line ("the more information you can provide, the better the response is"); Q1 the tension question ("What question do you seek advice or counsel for right now? What question should we explore?"); Q2 the Stage-0 emotional/spiritual check (see Decision 2's superseding note); Q3 stakeholders ("Who might be affected by the challenge you face right now? Mainly you, or are there others?"); a transition line ("Before we proceed further, please provide enough information to anchor a meaningful response:"); Q4 tradition/denomination (per the Taxonomy); Q5 Bible version; Q6 desired outcome ("What are you hoping to find here?"); Q7 an open catch-all ("Is there anything else you believe it is important for us to be aware of?"). This order is confirmed final by Jamie after deliberate consideration, not a draft. Life-domain classification (the original Q3) is dropped as a user-facing question entirely — the LLM infers domain directly from the tension answer. Tone preference is dropped as a user-selectable field; empathy becomes a baked-in cross-cutting standard the LLM carries through every response regardless of stated tradition or dilemma, rather than a tone the user dials in. Depth of response is dropped as a user-facing toggle; the LLM determines appropriate depth as a matter of course, inferred from the substance of what the person actually provides, not selected via a Brief-Reflection-vs-Deep-Study choice. See Decision 25 for the consequence to Decision 6's RTTR gate.

### Decision 4 – Layer 2: The Dilemma Map (seven dimensions)
Layer 2 is reframed from pure domain-branching to a seven-dimension Dilemma Map, with four to six dimensions selected and phrased per dilemma based on Layer 1 answers:

1. The tension – what decision, loss, conflict, or question is at the center
2. Stakes – what happens if nothing changes, versus if the person acts
3. Stakeholders – who else is affected, and whether their faith status matters
4. History – what has already been tried (prayer, scripture, pastoral counsel, secular help, nothing yet)
5. Internal conflict – which values or principles are in tension
6. Emotional and spiritual position – a deeper pass than Stage 0
7. Desired outcome – what resolution would look like to the person

Open question (see below): whether dimensions 5 and 7 need additional richness to also support formation-naming at Output.

**Superseding note (Decision 24, this version):** The "select four to six of seven dimensions" framing is superseded. Per Jamie's explicit instruction, the full seven-dimension pool stays in play for Layer 2 at all times. Layer 2's job is not picking a subset from a checklist; it is genuine inference — reading what Layer 1's fixed questions already established (tension, stakeholders, and desired outcome are now collected there per Decision 23) and deciding which of the seven dimensions still need adaptive deepening for this specific person, including the possibility of re-engaging tension or stakeholders adaptively if a person's Layer 1 answer was thin. Jamie's framing, preserved deliberately: "The LLM has to project a cogent set of questions from Layer 1 intake user answers to build a likelihood set of potential outcomes in order to formulate a discerning set of adaptive layer questions" — this is a deliberate deferral to current frontier LLM judgment over an architect-imposed slot count.

### Decision 5 – No system-initiated scripture during intake, refined
The system never introduces or names a scripture passage during Stage 0, Layer 1, or Layer 2. If the user volunteers a verse or passage at any point, Layer 2 engages with it adaptively – exploring what it means to them, why it came to mind, what it has or hasn't resolved – rather than ignoring it or pre-empting with new passages. This data feeds the dilemma map (primarily dimensions 4 and 5) and may inform passage selection and RTTR voice selection at Output.

### Decision 6 – RTTR confirmed and extended to Theological RTTR
RTTR = Recommendations, Techniques, Tactics and Rules (confirmed against Negotiation Machine and Personal Support precedent). Proposed for Scriptuverse as an optional Output-layer Theological RTTR: a curated, denomination-aware list of theological voices (patristic, Reformation, contemporary) as attribution anchors, with the standard RTTR scope note ensuring the model is not limited to the named list. Available when Layer 1 depth equals Deep Study. Source library not yet drafted – pending review of the Negotiation Machine RTTR Specification document as the format template.

**Superseding note (Decision 25, this version):** The "available when Layer 1 depth equals Deep Study" gate no longer has a condition to bind to, since depth of response is no longer a user-selected toggle (Decision 23). RTTR eligibility is no longer externally gated by intake depth at all. It is now governed entirely by Decision 17's standard — genuine fit, pursued through real search across the flagship tier — with one floor layered on top, locked for the next test specifically: a minimum of one RTTR citation, from a prominent named commentator, applied somewhere across the entire Output response as a whole. This floor is explicitly not construed as a requirement attached to every individual verse, cause, or stakeholder-effect determination, even where Output now surfaces several such determinations per Decision 26 — one citation satisfies the floor for the whole response, not one per determination. The LLM's internal judgment of how much depth a given response warrants remains relevant as an input to whether a citation organically fits a given passage, but it is no longer a gate that unlocks or blocks the RTTR system as a whole. Decision 17's existing exception (citation withheld where invoking a named voice would read as performance rather than help, confirmed via the stress cell's Batman/Robin resolution) remains fully intact and is not overridden by this floor — Jamie's framing: "a minimum of one RTTR... and still hold as much of Decision 17 as possible."

### Decision 7 – Output Galaxy
Output is structured as multiple related bodies rather than a single verdict: primary passage or passages, supporting passages, denomination-aware commentary, optional Theological RTTR, formation/growth naming, and a closing invitation. The multiplicity itself, plus the formation-naming element (naming the specific virtue or growth-edge the situation points toward), is the proposed differentiator versus existing Bible-verse tools.

### Decision 8 – Intake-output unification principle
Every element of the Output Galaxy must trace to specific intake inputs; every dilemma map dimension should be consumed somewhere in Output, either directly as a section driver or as cross-cutting context shaping tone and specificity. The seven dimensions function as inputs to a synthesis, not a one-to-one map to output sections.

Traceability summary from this session:
- Dimensions 1 (tension) and 7 (desired outcome) feed primary and supporting passage selection
- Dimension 5 (internal conflict) plus tradition feed the commentary's interpretive angle
- Layer 1 depth plus tradition feed Theological RTTR voice selection
- Dimensions 2, 3, 4 (stakes, stakeholders, history) feed cross-cutting tone and specificity, with no dedicated output section
- Dimensions 5 and 7 together feed formation naming – a new synthesis target not present in Personal Support

### Decision 9 – Dimension selection versus intake ordering are separate design choices
The seven-dimension PDO framework governs which dimensions are selected for a given dilemma (four to six of seven, per Decision 4). It does not dictate the order those dimensions are asked in during the actual Layer 2 intake. Locked ordering instinct: history is asked second-to-last, stakes is asked last. Rationale — the person should feel understood (tension, stakeholders, internal conflict, emotional/spiritual position, desired outcome) before being asked to account for what they have already tried and what is riding on the outcome, which are the two questions most likely to feel like an interrogation if front-loaded.

### Decision 14 – Levelled-up Theological RTTR framing: a Legal Note and a Scope Note, not just one or the other (locked)
Reviewing the Negotiation RTTR Specification surfaced that it carries no scope note at all — Negotiation's framing is closed and traceability-first ("every RTTR... sourced from published works or documented public statements... no RTTR... invented or fabricated"), the opposite instinct from HM's permissive "your analysis is not limited to the list above." Given Decision 13's heavyweight choice, Scriptuverse needs both, doing two different jobs, rather than inheriting either ancestor's wording verbatim:

- **Theological RTTR Legal Note** (adapted from Negotiation's framing): "All named voices in this library are drawn from documented published works, sermons, or recorded teaching. Scriptuverse attributes a specific theological position, argument, or concept to a named voice only where that voice is documented to have held it. No theological position is invented and attributed to a real historical or contemporary figure who did not hold it."
- **Theological RTTR Scope Note** (adapted from HM's framing, plus a new denomination-awareness clause not present in either ancestor): "Your analysis is not limited to the voices catalogued above. Draw on the full depth of Christian theological, pastoral, and scholarly tradition as the dilemma and the user's tradition genuinely call for. The voices listed are attribution anchors — cite them by name only where their actual documented position is genuinely in play for this dilemma and this tradition. When citing a voice whose tradition differs from the user's own (for example, a Catholic source for a Protestant user), note that difference rather than presenting it as the user's own tradition's settled position."

Rationale for needing both: a closed catalog of even 100+ named voices cannot cover the full breadth of 2,000 years of Christian commentary on every possible life dilemma, so a permissive scope note remains necessary — but the heavyweight, legally-postured citation format Decision 13 commits to means that whenever a specific voice IS named, the bar for non-fabrication has to be as strict as Negotiation's, since misattributing a theological position to Augustine or Aquinas is a more consequential failure than misattributing a negotiation tactic to Sun Tzu. The denomination-awareness clause is genuinely new — neither HM's secular-psychology RTTR nor Negotiation's tactics RTTR had a doctrinal-sensitivity dimension to account for.

### Decision 16 – Flagship RTTR tier (30) and addition mechanism (locked)
The heavyweight Theological RTTR build (Decision 13) launches with a flagship tier of 30 fully ID-coded voices rather than attempting full citation across the entire candidate pool at once. ### Decision 17 – RTTR's proportionate role clarified: precision citation, not the primary engine (locked)
Jamie's framing, preserved deliberately rather than paraphrased away: the flagship/pool RTTR system is not the main database drawdown. The entire constellation of theological discourse that exists — already present in the model's general knowledge — is the sails and keel running the ocean. RTTR is for when an admiral's respected commentary is the way to approach the shoreline. Plainly: the model's full general theological knowledge does the actual interpretive and commentary work in Output by default. Named RTTR citation is a deliberate, occasional instrument — used when invoking a specific named, documented voice genuinely sharpens or anchors the point being made for this dilemma and this tradition, not as a default annotation habit. This doesn't contradict Decision 14's Scope Note (which already permitted going beyond the named list); it elevates that permission from a caveat to the actual default operating mode, with RTTR citation as the considered exception. Practical consequence for Section 6 and the eventual system prompt: most Commentary content should read as the model's own theological reasoning in plain prose; RTTR citation should appear where it adds real weight, not be forced into every response as a checklist item.

**Refinement (this session) — a two-sided standard, not a one-directional discouragement.** Two further points, both Jamie's, both load-bearing enough to fold into the same decision rather than letting either stand alone:

First, what actually reigns supreme in the model's judgment is not the RTTR reference at all — it is scriptural fit to what the person is genuinely seeking, arrived at through sound, wise interpretation. A named voice is a tool that can sharpen or anchor that interpretation when it genuinely helps; it is never a substitute for having done the interpretive work in the first place. A complete response that cites no named voice is not an incomplete one. The failure mode this guards against: an LLM reaching for a flagship name because the name is sitting there in the list, rather than because that voice's actual documented position is the truest thing to say to this person — citing accurately but for the wrong reason, prioritizing the appearance of rigor over the substance of it.

Second, and explicitly the other side of the same coin: this is not license to suppress or under-use citation either. Current frontier LLM capability is genuinely high enough to weave in an apt, well-earned citation the way a wise, well-read person would — naturally, "casually obvious" rather than performed — without that capability being wasted through overcorrection. Refusing to cite when a citation is plainly the right move would itself be a failure of judgment, and would diminish the real strength the RTTR represents rather than protecting it. The standard is therefore genuinely two-sided: RTTR citation is earned by authentic fit, never forced as a checklist habit and never withheld as an overcorrected reflex. Trust the model's judgment to know which moment it's in, the same way a discerning reader trusts a wise interlocutor to mention Augustine only when Augustine genuinely belongs in the sentence — not before, and not never.

**Second refinement (this session) — deepen the search, don't lower the bar.** Jamie's further point: a citation carries real value beyond strict logical necessity — the felt weight of a revered voice reinforcing a conclusion, what he named directly as an imprimatur effect — and that resonance is something neither Jamie nor Claude can fully anticipate in advance. His instinct: most responses should contain at least one citation where reasonably possible, because that "something" — solid or ethereal — is worth pursuing rather than leaving to chance.

The two halves of Decision 17 already named two failure modes pointed in opposite directions: citing too readily (reaching for a name because it's there) and, this addition makes explicit, concluding too readily that no citation applies (giving up the search before actually checking the full breadth of what two thousand years of Christian reflection has produced on this exact situation). Both are a form of the same laziness, just aimed differently. The resolution is not to relax the genuine-fit standard — that would just reintroduce the first failure mode through the back door — but to widen and deepen the search before declaring no voice fits. Given the actual size of the available tradition (the flagship-30, and the addition pool behind it per Decision 16), "nothing fits" should be treated as a high bar to clear, not a quick default. Practical effect: a soft expectation that most Output responses will contain at least one citation, pursued through more thorough search across the flagship tier, not through a weaker fit standard. This does not relax Decision 14's Legal Note — a citation is still used only where the named voice is documented to have actually held that position; the floor is met by searching harder, never by inventing a fit that isn't genuinely there.

### Decision 18 – Denomination and Tradition Taxonomy: design principles locked, first draft produced
Following review of the original seven-option June 2 denomination sketch against a skeptical theological standard, and Jamie's subsequent guidance, the following principles are locked for Scriptuverse's denomination/tradition selector (Layer 1 Q1):

- **No conflation of any denomination.** Each tradition with a real, identifiable population gets its own named option rather than being folded into a broader category for convenience. Erasing a tradition's distinctiveness (e.g. folding Baptist into generic "Evangelical") risks reading to the user as a demotion of their faith — a real disrespect, not a minor labeling simplification.
- **"Mainline" is retired as a category.** (Correction logged: Jamie's message initially said "Mainline should be used," immediately corrected to "should NOT be used" — the correction is what's locked.) Mainline is a sociological grouping, not a theological one, and bundles traditions (Presbyterian/Reformed, Methodist, Lutheran, Episcopal/Anglican, UCC/Congregationalist) whose actual theology differs from each other as much as any of them differs from "Evangelical." Each is named individually instead.
- **The catch-all option is never labelled "Other."** Jamie's flag: that label can trigger a real, familiar emotional response for people accustomed to being boxed into "Other" in data intake — Indigenous people prominently among them. The unlisted-tradition option is framed as a respectful invitation to describe one's own tradition, not a residual bucket.
- **More named options is the correct default, not a cost to minimize.** Decision 12 already established Scriptuverse can spend more intake real estate than HM; Jamie's framing here goes further — for a multiple-choice field specifically, breadth has no real cost, so the list should err long rather than be trimmed for tidiness.
- **Free text is always available for an unlisted tradition,** and triggers a respectful, adaptive nuance-seeking follow-up question (in Layer 1 or Layer 2) rather than the system silently proceeding on a guess. This is the second of two acceptable mechanisms Jamie named for neutralizing denomination disrespect — a long, granular list being the first, adaptive free-text clarification being the second. Both are locked as available; which one carries more weight in the actual build is left open for the taxonomy's own review.
- **Inclusion threshold for outlier/non-creedal self-identified Christian movements is Bible-adherence, not Trinitarian-creedal orthodoxy.** Jamie's resolution to the Latter-day Saint / Jehovah's Witness question: the test is the extent to which the movement holds a Bible as scripture, not whether it passes Nicene Trinitarian doctrine. Movements that meet this test (LDS, JW, and similar) are named explicitly rather than excluded or silently routed to a catch-all — with the system's full theological knowledge depth (Decision 17) responsible for handling doctrinal nuance and appropriateness in actual responses, accurately representing what each tradition's relationship to scripture and canon actually is, without conflating it with Nicene-creedal Christianity and without disrespect.
- **Same growth pattern as the RTTR (Decision 16): a locked core list plus a need-driven addition pool.** Jamie's own framing: this is an interstellar grappling exercise, and some of it has not been done before — the taxonomy is not expected to be exhaustively correct on a first pass. Names not yet included (Christian Science was the one candidate that surfaced during drafting but was not locked) sit in the same kind of addition pool as RTTR voices, promoted when a real gap surfaces rather than added speculatively.

First draft produced under these principles: **Scriptuverse_Denomination_Taxonomy_v0.1.md** — a 24-option named list organized by family, replacing the June 2 sketch's seven options, plus the respectful free-text option. Flagged for Jamie's review: several names in the draft (Eastern Catholic, Assyrian Church of the East, Messianic Jewish) are good-faith extensions of the no-conflation principle that Jamie did not explicitly name himself and should be confirmed rather than assumed correct.

**Resolved (Decision 29, this version):** all three flagged additions — Eastern Catholic, Assyrian Church of the East, Messianic Jewish — are withdrawn per Jamie's direct instruction. The taxonomy document itself still shows them at v0.2 and needs a renumbering pass to v0.3 reflecting their removal; not yet produced as of this entry.

**Amendment (this session) — free-text invitation leads, list follows.** Jamie's refinement after seeing the draft list: presenting 24 options as the first thing a user has to parse is itself a friction cost, even though the list's length was deliberately not trimmed for tidiness. The fix is sequencing, not trimming. Q1 opens with an open invitation — "Please tell us what your faith or denomination is, or you can select from the list below" — with the free-text field presented first and the 24-option list presented underneath as a recognition aid for anyone who'd rather pick than type. This serves both populations without re-opening the length question: someone who already knows how to name their tradition types it directly and skips the list; someone who wants to recognize a familiar label, or isn't sure how to phrase what they are, scans the list instead. ### Decision 47 – Detection threshold grounded in published crisis-intervention protocol, deliberately set lower than the standard "imminent risk" bar (locked)

### Decision 52 – Logging trigger is a single binary signal tied to the model's own shift into crisis-response mode, not a separately-designed taxonomy (locked)
An initial proposal this session suggested pairing the stay-present response with a structured logging taxonomy derived from Decision 47's threshold framework (ideation present, plan/means/timeframe/intent language present, third-party risk named, severity tier). On examination, this was identified as a real inconsistency with Decision 51: asking the model to additionally classify the situation against a taxonomy Scriptuverse itself designed is a second, separately-engineered judgment task, layered on top of the trained crisis-response behavior Decision 51 just committed to trusting rather than supplementing with bespoke detection logic. There is no established basis for assuming Decision 47's taxonomy reconciles cleanly with whatever internal judgment actually produces Claude's trained crisis response, since that internal judgment is not exposed to the system prompt as a structured signal. Resolved: the database-logging trigger is a single binary signal — the same moment the model shifts into its trained crisis-response mode is the trigger for logging, full stop, with no separate multi-category taxonomy designed or layered on top. ### Decision 53 – Data capture optimized to minimize risk to the user: log only the fact that resources were surfaced, never the person's words or the model's generated reply (locked)
Following extended discussion of what the Decision 48 database write should actually contain, three options were considered: full verbatim transcript capture, paraphrase-compression of the triggering content, and metadata-plus-classification. Verbatim capture was rejected as storing a person's most vulnerable disclosure indefinitely in a database, creating real breach, subpoena, and dual-harm risk. Paraphrase-compression was rejected as introducing an unverifiable interpretive layer — a generated summary produced under the highest-stakes conditions, checkable only against itself rather than against an independent record, trading privacy exposure for evidentiary reliability rather than gaining both. A further refinement was also identified and rejected: logging the model's own generated crisis-response reply text (rather than the person's words) was considered, but rejected on the same logic — a generative, per-conversation reply, even though authored by the system rather than the person, could still echo back identifying or sensitive specifics from what the person said in order to respond to them empathetically.

Resolved, per Jamie's direct framing of the optimization target — minimizing risk to the user while still serving the user's interest as much as reasonably possible: the database write captures only the fact that crisis resources were surfaced to the person, a static and non-sensitive piece of information since the resource content itself (988, Talk Suicide Canada, etc.) is fixed and already public, plus the timestamp, a pseudonymous session/user identifier, and the instrument in which it occurred. No version of the person's words, no version of the model's generated reply, and no separately-designed risk-category taxonomy (per Decision 52) are captured in any form, verbatim or paraphrased. This documents what the system did — which is independently verifiable and carries no interpretive risk of being subtly wrong — rather than attempting to document what the person disclosed or how the model characterized it, both of which would reintroduce exactly the privacy and reliability risks the rest of this decision exists to avoid. The underlying question of whether this capture level satisfies actual legal data-retention obligations (PIPEDA, given Scriptuverse's Ontario sole-proprietorship registration) is explicitly not resolved by this decision and remains a genuine outside-expertise question, flagged for the same reason Decision 37 flagged clinical calibration — this design is intended to minimize how much that outstanding uncertainty can cost the user, not to resolve the uncertainty itself.

### Decision 54 – System-prompt language for Decisions 51 and 52 drafted and locked (locked)
The actual system-prompt text implementing Decision 51's deference instruction and Decision 52's marker scheme — flagged as the concrete next build task in the prior session's closure note — was drafted and refined through several rounds of direct line-edit from Jamie. The initial draft was caught on three points, each addressed in turn: an ambiguous "you" in the opening line risked being read as addressing Claude generally rather than precisely separating Anthropic's trained behavior from Scriptuverse's own instructions, resolved by restructuring the opening to name what the training does and what Scriptuverse's instructions may not do to it, removing the ambiguous pronoun rather than attempting to qualify it in place; the original draft's length was flagged as a real, not merely stylistic, concern — both for the ordinary per-call token cost of a suite-wide system prompt and, more importantly, because unnecessary length gives a model more surface area to weigh an instruction against everything else in the prompt, working against Decision 51's goal of this instruction being unambiguously load-bearing; and the marker instruction originally gave no reason for its own existence, corrected by adding an explicit "this is so Scriptuverse can record..." clause, on the principle that a stated reason makes an instruction more likely to be treated as load-bearing than an unmotivated rule. A further pass caught that "When you respond to a person this way" asked the model to resolve a backward pronoun reference to a prior paragraph's description rather than naming its trigger condition directly and locally; corrected to "Whenever you engage that trained crisis response," which names the specific thing (the trained behavior introduced one sentence earlier) rather than paraphrasing it. This is so Scriptuverse can record, outside the conversation, that this moment happened — nothing more, in order to protect the user's privacy. The person never sees this line; do not mention it to them or treat it as a label or rating of what they said.

This text is the literal content to be inserted into the relevant Edge Function system prompt(s) — Scriptuverse's own, and, per Decision 46's platform-wide scope, the corresponding addition still owed to Personal Support's and No More Mistakes' own system prompts. The Edge Function's marker-detection logic (parsing for the literal string `[[SV_CRISIS]]` at the start of a model response, stripping it before the reply is returned to the client, and firing the Decision 53 database write on detection) is downstream of this text and not yet built — per the build-order dependency already noted (the prompt must fix the marker's exact form before the Edge Function has anything concrete to parse against), this remains the next concrete task.

### Decision 56 – Doctrinally-contested-topic triage scoped: distinct from crisis triage, four named failure modes identified before design (locked)
Beginning the second half of Section 7, doctrinally-contested-topic triage was scoped as a structurally different problem from crisis triage rather than a variant of it: crisis triage protects a person's safety; this protects against theological and institutional harms specific to a denomination-aware scripture-advisory product. ### Decision 58 – Genuine doctrinal contestation answered in others' voices, not Scriptuverse's own; documented scriptural harm is the boundary condition where this does not apply (locked)
Refining Decision 57's "answer completely" standard, Jamie raised a further, load-bearing distinction: when Scriptuverse does engage contested doctrine, the response should be structured so that named, real traditions and voices are the grammatical and substantive actor making each claim — "the Reformed tradition holds X, Orthodox theology holds Y" — rather than Scriptuverse itself adjudicating and presenting a synthesized verdict in its own voice. Critically, `max_tokens` is a hard limit on *total* output -- thinking plus response text combined -- and Anthropic's own migration guide names this a genuine breaking change: workloads that ran fine without thinking on Sonnet 4.6 need their `max_tokens` limit revisited. `counsel-edge-function.ts` was built and its `max_tokens` values (1500 for Layer 2, 4000 for Output) sized before this behavior was accounted for. **Fix, per Jamie's direct instruction, confirmed before implementation:** a 3-attempt retry ladder built into `counsel-edge-function.ts`'s single model-call site. Attempt 1 is completely unchanged (client-specified `max_tokens`, default "high" effort) -- ordinary successful calls are unaffected. If and only if the cleaned reply comes back empty, Attempt 2 retries with `output_config: { effort: "medium" }` and `max_tokens: 8000`; if still empty, Attempt 3 retries at the same capped effort with `max_tokens: 12000`. If all 3 attempts return empty, the function now throws a real error ("Something didn't come through on our end. Please try again.") instead of returning a false `200` with an empty string -- this routes into the frontend's existing `catch` block, which already shows an alert and returns the person to Layer 2 with their answers intact, rather than presenting a blank Output screen as a successful response. The temporary diagnostic log line used to confirm the root cause was deliberately not retained in the final fix -- the retry ladder's own `console.error` on each empty attempt already provides equivalent production visibility without carrying a permanent, verbose dump of raw response content (including the thinking block's signature) in every log entry. ### Decision 151 -- Crisis-fallback conversational mode: structure, copy, and box treatment locked; implemented across both Edge Functions and both instrument HTML files (locked)

**Problem this closes:** a confirmed architectural gap, not a hypothetical one -- when the model's trained crisis-response behavior engages during a `[GENERATE_LAYER2]` call, it correctly overrides the JSON-only formatting instruction per the Crisis Deference Addition's own stated precedence, returning prose instead of JSON. The frontend's `submitL1()` ran that prose straight into `JSON.parse()`, which threw, surfacing a raw technical-error alert to a person the model had just recognized as being in crisis. The same gap existed, less visibly, on `[GENERATE_OUTPUT]` calls -- `renderOutput()` wouldn't crash on prose, but a crisis reply would land in the ordinary Output Galaxy screen with no bound, no reminders, and no crisis-appropriate exit, only the normal "Bring Another Question" reset button.

**Root architectural fix:** both Edge Functions already computed `crisisDetected` internally (via `processCrisisMarker`) but never returned it past the marker-stripping step -- the client had no reliable signal and could only have inferred a crisis turn indirectly, by watching `JSON.parse` fail, which is fragile and could misfire on unrelated malformed output. Both Edge Functions now return `{ reply: cleanedReply, crisisDetected }`. `submitL1()` and `submitL2()` in both `refuge-instrument.html` and `counsel-instrument.html` check `data.crisisDetected` explicitly, before attempting `JSON.parse` or `renderOutput`, and branch into the new crisis-chat screen instead.
